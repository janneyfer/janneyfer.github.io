Paracentesis

Supplies

1 bouffant cap 1 mask 1 pair of sterile gloves 3 chloropreps
22-25 gauge needle Ultrasound Lumbar puncture kit Skin marking pen
1% or 2% lidocaine 10ml syringe 60ml syringe high-pressure drainage tubing
Over-the-needle catheter Specimen tubes Large evacuated containers Sterile occlusive dressing

Steps

Looking for fluid pocket

  1. Position the patient in slight decubitus supine position when looking for a pocket along the right or left lower abdominal quadrants. Look for pockets lateral to the rectus sheath to avoid puncturing the interior epigastric artery
  2. Using the low frequency probe (square) look for a pocket of fluid (appears black). Rotate the probe 90 degrees to look at the two-dimensional space. Circle your target space with a surgical marker
  3. Using the high frequency probe (rectangle) look at the subcutaneous space for any vessels. Mark the location of the vessels
  4. Repeat steps 2 and 3 at different sites looking for the best pocket with no overlying vessels
  5. Avoid areas of dilated vessels and infection

Preparing the patient and your equipment

  1. Clean the site with chloroprep twice
  2. Open up your sterile paracentesis kit. Using a filter needle, draw up 10cc of lidocaine. Switch the filter needle for a 22-25 gauge needle
  3. Tuck a white paper drape under the patient
  4. Place the drape with the circle opening centered on your spot
  5. Place blue towels to the right, left, top, and bottom of the spot

Paracentesis

  1. Using lidocaine, create a wheal. Go in perpendicular to the skin carefully pull back as you slowly advance to anesthetize the underlying tissue. Aspirate until yellow ascites fluid returns. Inject lidocaine into the peritoneal space before withdrawing the needle
  2. For therapeutic taps
    • Put together the over the needle cathether. Make sure the catheter and needle snap together. There is a safety mechanism on the catheter with an indicator flashing red when the needle is pushing against the skin and subcutaneous tissue. The device will turn green when you are in the peritoneal cavity
    • Insert the catheter needle into the same track created with the lidocaine needle. Intermittently pull back on the plunger as you continue to advance the needle. Once ascitic fluid returns, stop advancing and guide the catheter into the peritoneal space and remove the needle
    • Attach the high pressure tubing to the end of the cathteter and the other end to a vacuum container or the wall suction tube
  3. For diagnostic ONLY taps
    • Insert the needle into the same track as the lidocaine needle. Pull back intermittently as you advance the needle until ascitic fluid returns.
    • Attach a larger syringe and aspirate 30-60cc of fluid for diagnostic testing
  4. Once the desired fluid has been drained, remove the catheter and apply dermadond over the site. Cover with gauze and tegaderm.
