Temporary triple lumen catheter

  • Placed emergently for quick venous access
  • Short term use only 7-14 days
  • Placed directly into the internal jugular, subclavian or femoral vein

Non-tunneled: PICCs

  • Stands for peripherally inserted central catheters
  • Inserted into the basilic, cephalic, or brachial vein, advanced until the tip is in the superior vena cava
  • Can have one, two, or three separate lumens

Tunneled: Hickman and Broviac

  • Tunneled under the skin and surgically placed in the chest
  • Enters the subclavian vein and rests in the SVC
  • A broviac is smaller and used in pediatrics
