Bone Marrow Biopsy

Supplies

1 bouffant cap 1 mask 1 pair of sterile gloves
2 chloropreps 4 sterile blue towels 1 chlorhexidine (optional)
1 bone marrow biopsy kit 1 bone marrow drill

Steps

Prepping the patient and your equipment

  1. If performing the biopsy of the right posterior iliac crest, then have the patient lie on the left lateral decub. Place chuck under patient.
  2. Give IV sedation in slow increments using versed and fentanyl
  3. To locate the posterior iliac crest, place your fourth finger on the anterior superior iliac spine, and reach down vertically with an outstretched hand towards the patient's spine. The posterior iliac crest is where your thumb falls. Feel with your thumb for a ridge. Mark the spot with a purple surgical marker.
  4. Clean the area twice with chloroprep
  5. Gown up and put on sterile gloves
  6. Tuck sterile drape at the bottom of the patient's back. Using chloroprep clean the skin overlying the posterior iliac crest. Place sterile blue towels to the left and right of your field. Place your sterile drape centering the hole at the area you marked.

Aspiration and biopsy

  1. Draw up 10cc of lidocaine and create a wheal at your marked site. Insert the lidocaine needle in, feel for bone at needle end, and inject to provide local anesthesia. Tip: if you cannot feel bone from excess subcutaneous tissue, switch your 24 gauge needle for a spinal needle
  2. Using a blade, make a horizontal skin at the marked site
  3. Locate the posterior iliac crest with your left hand. Use your left hand for proprioception to help guide the bone marrow needle. With the right hand insert the bone marrow needle until you feel bone. You are in the posterior iliac crest if the patient moves with applied pressure to the needle. Tip: If the bone gives away, then you may be over calcified cortex. Redirect the needle to look for the iliac crest
  4. Drill until you hear a change in frequency, or feel a change in pressure. Remove the drill, and unscrew the center bone marrow needle. With a syringe aspirate 10-15cc of bone marrow. Look for bone spicules to confirm quality of aspirate
  5. For the biopsy you will need to redirect your bone marrow needle. Reapply the center bone marrow needle. Drill the needle 3mm out, redirect the needle to the 10 o' clock position, and re-drill in about 3mm
  6. Remove the center bone marrow needle. In one swift movement, drill in 1-2cm and rapidly drill out removing the entire needle. Collect your core biopsy
  7. Apply pressure to the biopsy site for 15 mins. Dress with sterile gauze and tegaderm
